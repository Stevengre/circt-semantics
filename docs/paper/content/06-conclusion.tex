\section{Conclusion}

This paper introduced K-CIRCT, a formal semantics framework for CIRCT. Our semantics is easy to use with a C++ interface to stimulate hardware modules. The validation of our extensible semantics shows that the already constructed core dialects semantics are correct and enough for real-world hardware representation. Furthermore, applications show our formal semantics potential for real-world hardware verification, especially for CIRCT to validate its compilation. For future work, we plan to work with the CIRCT and K communities to explore and improve the scalability of K-CIRCT, providing a rigorous foundation and useful formal tools for CIRCT.
